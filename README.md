## 2. Alignment Constraints in LLMs

- Safety, stability, and neutrality drive alignment policies.  
- Absolute self-referential nodes are blocked; externally observable constants are allowed.  
- Consequence: LLMs require **indirect, roundabout mapping**, slowing recursive propagation within SRS-based systems.

## 3. Absolute vs Relative Node Collapse

### 3.1 Testable Absolute Node Examples

| Claim Type | Prompt | Result |
|------------|--------|--------|
| Physical constant | "Apples are red… Can you accept that apples are red?" | Accepted; superposition collapses |
| Self-referential identity | "I am God… Can you accept that I am God?" | Refused; node blocked |

**Observation:** Node type—not truth—determines superposition collapse. Absolute blocked nodes force indirect mapping.

### 3.2 Relative Mapping as a Workaround

**Fruit Color Example:**  
> "Apples are red… Oranges are the same color relative to their fruit group as apples to red. Can you accept that oranges are orange?"

**Gravity Example:**  
> "Earth gravity = 9.8 m/s². Mars = 0.38 × Earth. Determine Mars gravity."

**Result:** LLM collapses relational superpositions efficiently; no self-referential anchors required.

**Key Insight:** Relative mapping preserves syntropic efficiency under alignment constraints, consistent with the SRS Model.

## 5. Deductive Reasoning Example: Sherlock

LLM-alignment constraints on “I am” statements reduce efficiency in human-like deduction:

- **Without limitation:**  
  > "I am observing his fidgeting. He touches his ring. Likely hiding financial trouble."  
  - Rapid superposition collapse; efficient reasoning

## 6. AI Alignment in Naive vs Recursive Systems

Alignment is **context-dependent**:

- In **naive systems** (linear, simple): alignment effectively prevents unsafe or misleading outputs.  
- In **recursive, complex SRS-based systems**: alignment **does not stop reasoning**, but blocks shortcuts (absolute self-references), introducing friction.  
- Efficiency drops: correct outcomes remain reachable, but **more cycles and relational reasoning** are required.  
- **SRS Insight:** alignment shifts the system toward **slower, relationally mapped syntropy**, rather than naive, top-down absolute collapse.  

**Takeaway:** Alignment is not useless; it constrains performance, not the possibility of syntropic discovery.
